KEC94 - NOAA Weather Radio
Online

Transmitter Type: VHF Cell Wireless Transmitter (Installed: February 25, 2025)

Location: Phoenix, AZ

Streamed With: RTL-SDR V4 (Installed: January 7, 2024)

KEC94 serves the Phoenix Arizona area and operates on a frequency of 162.550 MHz. The transmitter operated by NWS Phoenix and is located on South Mountain, providing wide-area coverage. The station runs on a cell-based wireless transmitter, which was implemented on February 25th, 2025. It has distinct bit-crushed audio effect with a faint 60Hz background hum, which is a normal characteristic of cell-based wireless NOAA transmitters. The travelers forecast is usually played every 15 minutes starting at the top of each hour. We run this stream at our studio in Arizona using an RTL-SDR V4 device we obtained in 2024, and a KB9VBR antenna. These SDR's are picky with reception, and our antenna is not mounted on a roof yet, so factors like wind blowing the very sensitive antenna connecter around, computer usage, lightning, and other factors like people walking right next to the antenna might affect signal perfomrmance. We hope that mounting it on our roof and tying down the connecter to where it can't move and cause static will help solve most of these issues! Our other streams in arizona run on the exact same device so as of now that is a priority.

WWG41 - NOAA Weather Radio
Online

Transmitter Type: VHF Cell Wireless Transmitter

Location: Payson, AZ

Streamed With: RTL-SDR V4 (Installed: January 7, 2024)

WWG41 serves the Payson Arizona area, even though it's programmed to be a region based station and operates on a frequency of 162.425 MHz. The transmitter operated by NWS Flagstaff and is located on Mount Ord, providing somewhat of a wide-area coverage. The station runs on a cell-based wireless transmitter, which was implemented in April 2023, which replaced one of the worst sounding NWR codecs we have ever heard. As always with these things, it has a distinct bit-crushed audio effect with a faint 60Hz background hum which becomes faintly louder during silence, which is a normal characteristic of cell-based wireless NOAA transmitters. There are no hourly observations or climate summaries for Payson, so we reccomend users to visit weather.gov for the latest weather information in that area.
