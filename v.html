V

Maroon 5
5/10
Released on Sep 2, 2014 | Reviewed on Sep 9, 2014

A Basic Pop Project

What can a young one do on a tuesday at school? Nothing! That's why Jayson Doutree has taken his time to review this album. V was released on September 2, 2014, and it’s clear that this project marks a pivotal moment in the band’s evolution, or even devolution, depending on your taste. Spanning 12 tracks and clocking in at just over 40 minutes, V leans heavily into this radio friendly pop scheme that you year on 100.3 The Peak all the time, extremely different from the funky rock of their debut album Songs About Jane. This project features what I think are gonna be radio hits like Maps and Sugar and Animals, which already seem to be getting popular, I feel as if these songs are gonna be plaguing Albuquerque airwaves in a matter of days. Maps is one of the best tracks on this project with its cool ass guitar riffs and upbeat tempo, makes it sound like I'm living in SoCal.

However, there are a lot of issues I have with this project. V is one of the most basic pop albums to come out of the 2010's so far. Tracks like Animals and Feelings rely heavily on a basic formula, relying on repetitive synth beats and Levine’s basic ass vocals, which lacks the common elements found on earlier projects. The production, while clean and radio ready, often feels like it is missing the organic instrumentation that once had this band rolling in the early 2000's. Songs like It Was Always You attempt to inject some emotional weight, but the extremely polished sound undermines any genuine messages. This is for sure a project that is it is playing it safe, as it seems to be prioritizing commercial success made for corporate companies like starbucks over artistic growth. It’s an album that’s easy to listen to but hard to love deeply, leaving a feeling of missed potential.

Rating

The 5/10 rating on the Doutree Richter scale shows a middle ground perspective on this project. On one hand, the album delivers polished, radio friendly pop that’s easy to listen to and hard to escape from (literally). Songs like Maps and Sugar are effective at This, with beats that stay stuck in your head for a long time. On the other hand, V lacks any sort of creativity that made Maroon 5 stand out years ago. The shift to a more generic pop sound feels like a trade off, sacrificing creativity for radio play. This project is a corporate sounding cash grab which does not shake up the ground that much, or get the club rolling which is why this V gets a 5/10 on the Doutree Richter Scale.

The album's commercial success, debuting at #1 on the Billboard 200 with 298,000 units moved in its first week, shows Carti still has a strong fanbase, but the critical divide suggests it may be more of a fan favorite than a universal hit. Carti failed to deliver on this project, which is why I am giving this project a light 4 on the Doutree Richter scale.

Favorite Tracks: Maps, Sugar, Animals

Least Favorite Track: Feelings
